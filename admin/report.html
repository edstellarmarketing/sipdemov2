THE ONE-LINE READ
NovaTech is a financially elite, culturally strong organisation with a critically misaligned capability infrastructure — delivering top-quartile financial returns on a below-median L&D foundation that will break before the next growth phase completes.
Diagnostic — What All 44 Indices Say as a System
Across 10 domains, four structural patterns emerge. Pattern 1 — Financial outperformance built on a lean workforce: REI (96), NRI (95), RGI (91), and GMI (88) are all top-quartile — but every financial metric is a function of 50 people. The organisation has no financial buffer against a capability shock. Pattern 2 — Complete framework, incomplete execution: SCAI (100%) means the skills architecture is fully built. SCR (58%) means it is barely half-trained. ACI (40%) means only 40% of the workforce has been assessed. The org has invested in infrastructure without investment in evidence. Pattern 3 — Training volume without strategic alignment: LPI (92%) confirms 100% training completion. LAI (34%) confirms 66% of training hours are misaligned with critical skills. NovaTech is one of the best-trained organisations in the region at doing the wrong things. Pattern 4 — Client health masking CS capacity risk: CHI (88), CGI (84), CQI (82) are all green — but 72 clients supported by 12 CS staff is a ratio that will snap under the next wave of enterprise onboarding. The composite score of 74/100 places NovaTech in the Strong band — but the band conceals a 10-domain split: Financial/Client/Culture average 86/100 (top quartile); Skill/L&D/Assessment average 58/100 (below peer median). These are two organisations in one operating model, and the gap is growing.
